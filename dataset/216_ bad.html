HISTORY OF JACK RUSSELL TERRIER

The Jack Russell Terrier, a spirited and charismatic breed, has a captivating history that dates back to the early 19th century in England. Its origin is closely linked to the efforts of one remarkable man, Reverend John “Jack” Russell, who played a pivotal role in shaping this breed’s distinctive traits and purpose. In the early 1800s, Reverend John Russell, a passionate fox hunter and a parson in Dartmouth, England, set out to develop the perfect hunting companion. He sought a dog that could fearlessly pursue foxes into their dens, a task that demanded exceptional agility, determination, and intelligence. With this vision in mind, he started breeding dogs that would eventually become known as Jack Russell Terriers. Russell was particularly fond of a small white Terrier named Trump, who became the foundation of his breeding program. Trump possessed all the qualities Reverend Russell admired in a hunting dog fearlessness, quickness, and an unyielding spirit. Trump’s offspring were carefully selected and bred to continue the line, ultimately leading to the establishment of the Jack Russell Terrier. The breed’s development focused on producing working dogs that excelled in fox hunting. Their small size allowed them to easily fit into narrow fox dens and burrows, while their powerful build and strong legs enabled them to pursue the foxes relentlessly. The Jack Russell Terriers would “go to ground,” meaning they would chase the fox underground and bark to alert the hunters of their location. As fox hunting grew in popularity in England, the demand for these skillful terriers increased. The breed’s tenacity and effectiveness in the field made them highly sought-after companions for hunters and farmers alike. Throughout the 19th century, the Jack Russell Terrier gained a reputation for its unwavering work ethic and innate hunting abilities. The breed’s recognition and formalization as the Jack Russell Terrier came much later, with the establishment of breed standards in the 20th century. The Kennel Club in the United Kingdom recognized the breed in 1989, and the American Kennel Club followed suit in 2000, classifying the breed as the Parson Russell Terrier to differentiate it from variants with different sizes and coat types. In modern times, Jack Russell Terriers have transitioned from primarily being working dogs to beloved companions in many households worldwide.

About Jack Russell Terrier

The Jack Russell Terrier, also called the Parson Russell Terrier, was developed in England some 200 years ago to hunt fox in the south of England. Parson John Russell, “Jack” to his friends, wanted an efficient hunting dog and decided to design exactly the dog he had in mind. The result was a bold, athletic dog who won hearts with his quickness, intelligence, determination, and intense desire to hunt. Today, Jack Russell Terriers are favorites among horse owners, dog sports enthusiasts, animal trainers for film and television, and people who simply appreciate their fearless personality, boundless energy, entertaining antics, and portable size. However, before you get a Jack Russell Terrier, it’s important to know that they are not for everyone. They can be a handful to train and manage, and they require a lot of attention, exercise, and supervision. Their vibrant personalities, boundless energy, and unwavering loyalty have won the hearts of countless dog lovers. As family pets, they bring joy and excitement to their owners, always ready for playtime and adventures. Despite their transformation into companion animals, the hunting instincts and high energy levels of Jack Russell Terriers remain deeply ingrained. As a result, they thrive in active environments where they can participate in agility courses, flyball, and other dog sports. Regular exercise and mental stimulation are essential to keep these intelligent dogs content and well-behaved. In addition to their physical prowess, Jack Russell Terriers are known for their bold and confident demeanor. They have a charming stubborn streak that can make training a challenge for novice dog owners. However, with consistent and positive reinforcement training methods, they can become obedient and well-mannered companions. Due to their intelligence and eagerness to please, Jack Russell Terriers are also used in various dog-assisted therapies. Their affectionate and friendly nature allows them to form deep connections with people, making them excellent therapy dogs for individuals in need of emotional support. Jack Russells vary widely in size, ranging in height from 10 to 15 inches at the shoulder and weighing 13 to 17 pounds, because different types were used for different purposes and terrain. Shorty Jacks, known as Jack Russells who stand 10 to 12 inches and are longer than they are tall, resemble Corgis or Dachshunds more than the taller, more balanced American Kennel Club-registered Parson Russell Terriers or the dogs registered by the Jack Russell Terrier Club of America or the Jack Russell Terrier Association of America.

OUR STORY

Thank you for visiting our website. I am called Devon Smith. The Devon’s family are the owners of Jack Russell Terrier Kennel. We have been breeding this lovely breed dogs since 2013. My wife and I are pet lovers so we took up the initiative to breed these lovely dog breed to satisfy the needs of families who will want to own or add a new member to their family. My knowledge about the Jack Russell Terrier  has grown as the years go by as they are a dog breed that are very smart and make us to learn new things everyday. My history with owning a puppy goes right back to my Christmas holidays when I was 10 years old. My parents bought me a lovely Jack Russell Terrier puppy which I named Rosie. Rosie gave me a lot of childhood memories when I was growing as a teen. She was more than a best friend to me. She was so lovely and friendly. Each time I got angry back then about something, Rosie was always there to console me and make me smile again. I always thought she could talk as she understood me so much. I always speak to her at times hoping she will respond. Everywhere I went to, Rosie was with me. I met my wife in college. We were very good friends back then. She loved Rosie until she insisted her parents should get her a Jack Russell Terrier puppy. That was how our love for this Intelligent  dog breed grew up. Our dogs use to play together. Unfortunately in our later years at college, I lost my lovely puppy Rosie. We grew up loving each other so much and loving this dog breed. Later on, we got married and with our love for Jack Russell Terrier. We decided to breed and raise this dog breed and why not educate puppy lovers about the importance of owning a Jack Russell Terrier. Fast forward to today, my wife and I raise our 3 kids and breed the Puppies on our 3 acres of land where our puppies have enough space to play around. My family and I are dedicated to help everyone own the perfect, healthy puppy for their home. With our knowledge of this dog breed from childhood, we will say Jack Russell Terrier are the best dogs to own. We spend our time in training these puppies and making sure we help families own the most intelligent of this dog breed.
