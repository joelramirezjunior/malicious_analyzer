CBD OIL INFORMATION

Buying CBD Oil in Australia

Buying CBD oil in Australia is legal. If a cannabidiol product is approved by the TGA and registered on the ARTG, then you can buy CBD oil from a pharmacist from behind the counter. They must meet the standards of a schedule 3 cannabidiol product, which include:

  • CBD makes up 98% or more of the total cannabinoid content
  • Other cannabinoids present must be naturally found in cannabis
  • If THC is present (the high-inducing cannabinoid) it can only take up 1% of the total cannabinoid content
  • Daily dose is 150mg max. with packs containing no more than 30 days’ supply.


When medicine is classed as schedule 3, it means the product is safe to use with professional advice and that you can purchase the medicine without a prescription from a pharmacist.  

CBD OIL USES

What is CBD used for?

Research into the effects and uses of CBD, THC and other cannabinoids is ongoing. Both here in Australia and overseas, many studies are being undertaken to look into the medicinal use of medical marijuana or weed.

At the moment, CBD is believed to have analgesic, anti-inflammatory and anti-psychoactive properties. CBD can be consumed orally as oils or liquid capsules. In these forms, the CBD is absorbed at a slower rate compared to other forms of accessible medical cannabis in Australia. The effects can last 8 – 24 hours, which means CBD Oil may be good for reducing symptoms of more chronic medical conditions that do not require immediate relief. Many studies have shown results that suggest CBD may help with anxiety, depression, PTSD, and insomnia.  

Low-dose CBD oils are available as pharmacy-only medicines, purchased from behind the counter. Supply may be limited or non-existent for a few years, but a registered schedule 3 CBD product is much safer than products that are bought from the black market and are unlikely to be contaminated.
