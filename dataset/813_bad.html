    Welcome to Garfield Ragdoll Cattery

    Familiar with this Breed?

    The Ragdoll is a large, gentle breed of cat known for its striking blue eyes, soft and fluffy coat, and affectionate personality. This breed was developed in California in the 1960s by a woman named Ann Baker, who bred a white Persian cat with a Birman cat to create a new breed that exhibited the best traits of both.

    One of the defining features of the Ragdoll breed is its calm and loving temperament. Ragdolls are known for being extremely affectionate and often seek out human company. They are sociable cats that enjoy being around people and are known to be great with children and other pets. They are also intelligent cats that can be trained to do tricks and respond to commands.

    Another notable characteristic of the Ragdoll breed is their soft and fluffy coat. They have long, silky hair that comes in a variety of colors, including seal, blue, chocolate, and lilac. Their coat requires regular grooming to prevent matting and tangling, but their gentle temperament makes them easy to handle during grooming sessions.

    Ragdolls are also known for their size. They are large cats that can weigh up to 20 pounds and can reach up to 3 feet in length. Despite their size, they are not particularly active cats and prefer to spend their time lounging and cuddling with their owners.

    Overall, the Ragdoll is a beloved breed of cat that is appreciated for its gentle personality, soft and fluffy coat, and affectionate nature. Whether you are looking for a loyal companion or a family pet, the Ragdoll is a popular choice that is sure to bring joy to your life.

    Love Your Pet

    Ragdoll's Personality

    The Ragdoll is a breed of cat known for its gentle and affectionate personality. These cats are often described as “lap cats” due to their love of cuddling and affection from their owners.

    One of the defining characteristics of the Ragdoll’s personality is its calm and docile nature. They are easy-going cats that are not easily startled or agitated. This makes them great pets for families with children or other pets. They are known for being patient and tolerant, even in situations that may be stressful for other cats.

    Ragdolls are also known for their affectionate nature. They are often described as dogs in cat bodies due to their loyalty and devotion to their owners. They love to be around people and are not shy about asking for attention. They are known to follow their owners around the house and even greet them at the door when they come home.

    Despite their size, Ragdolls are not particularly active cats. They are content to spend their time lounging and cuddling with their owners. They are not known for being climbers or jumpers, preferring to stay close to the ground. This makes them great pets for those who may not have the space or energy to keep up with a more active cat breed.

    Ragdolls are also intelligent cats that can be trained to do tricks and respond to commands. They are quick learners and enjoy the stimulation of training sessions. This makes them great pets for those who want a cat that can be taught to do more than just sit in their lap.

    Overall, the Ragdoll’s personality is one of the breed’s most appealing traits. Their gentle and affectionate nature, combined with their loyalty and intelligence, make them great pets for families and individuals alike. If you are looking for a cat that will be a loyal companion and a loving part of your family, the Ragdoll is a breed that should definitely be considered.

    Color Coat and Grooming

    “Maine coons have a heavy, shaggy coat which requires maintenance brushing to ensure it doesn’t get tangled or matted,” says Catherine Lenox, DVM, DACVN, a board certified veterinary nutritionist with Royal Canin.

    Your Maine coon will need regular, dedicated grooming and will require anywhere from weekly to monthly bathing. Their long coats are usually silky smooth, but when they start to look greasy or stringy, it’s time for a bath. They also require weekly brushing to keep their long hair and undercoat from getting tangled and matted. These cats do shed quite a bit, and regular brushing will also help get rid of loose hairs. Don’t worry—the Maine coon loves any sort of attention it can get, so grooming is usually a pleasant task.

    Maine coons don’t have any special exercise needs—they’ll keep the same activity schedule of most cats, with long hours of sleep and playful bouts of jumping and running through the house. Cat toys and cat trees can help them work out some of that energy.

    These super-intelligent cats are easily trainable. Some owners have described the Maine coon as “dog-like” for their ability to be trained—they can learn to walk on a leash outdoors or even play a round of fetch. They take quickly to basic housetraining and litter box use, but keep in mind these large cats will require a large litter box to help them comfortably do their business.
